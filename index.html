Oct. 16, 2023
We are honoring scholars who are shaping our community through their dedication and skill as teachers and mentors. We're also celebrating a first-ever win by a CUNY researcher of an NIH Pioneer award. You won't want to miss the inspiring story of alumna Melina Giakoumis and her whirlwind summer. Plus, see highlights from events featuring Walter Isaacson's insightful talk about his new biography of Elon Musk and "Oppenheimer" filmmakers Christopher Nolan and Emma Thomas discussing their film adaptation of Kai Bird's biography.
MEET THE TEACHING AND MENTORING AWARD WINNERS
Six students and professors are recognized for their outstanding contributions to CUNY classrooms and to graduate education. 
MANDË HOLFORD WINS PRESTIGIOUS NIH PIONEER AWARD
The first-ever CUNY researcher selected for the award, Holford will receive $5.5 million to explore new directions for using snail venoms and their evolutionary dynamics in drug discovery.
‘SCIENCE STOPS FOR NO WOMAN’
Alumna Melina Giakoumis explains how she balances motherhood, research, and a new job at the American Museum of Natural History.
CHRISTOPHER NOLAN, EMMA THOMAS ON ‘OPPENHEIMER’
The director and producer discussed with author Kai Bird how they transformed his Pulitzer Prize-winning biography into a blockbuster film.
‘NOT ALL STEEL IS STAINLESS’: WALTER ISAACSON ON MUSK
Elon Musk’s drive and penchant for risk, which have made him a daring inventor, are co-dependent on his callousness and the other loathsome aspects of his personality. Extract his dark side and the light would fade too, Walter Isaacson said as part of the Annual Leon Levy Biography Lecture. Read excerpts from his sold-out talk.
ENGLISH PROFESSORS SWEEP VICTORIAN BOOK AWARDS
Recognition from the North American Victorian Studies Association for Distinguished Professor Talia Schaffer and Professor Tanya Agathocleous highlights scholarship in Victorian studies at the Graduate Center.

A PUBLIC GRADUATE SCHOOL IN THE CENTER OF NYC

The Graduate Center of The City University of New York, a leader in public graduate education, is committed to doctoral and master's education for the public good.

Innovative research, rigorous scholarship, and collaborative and interdisciplinary learning define graduate education here and prepare students with creative, problem-solving expertise to thrive in and beyond the academy.

We are dedicated to CUNY's historic mission of educating the "children of the whole people," providing access to graduate education for diverse and highly talented students, including those who have been underrepresented in higher education.
