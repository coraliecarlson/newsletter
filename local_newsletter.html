Sept. 18, 2022
The Graduate Center has new leadership – from our new interim president to our alumni and faculty who are heading up groups that focus on Latino culture and Latin America. Learn more about them and meet some of our new students, who come to the Graduate Center with a breadth of experience, including a filmmaker, a heritage speaker of an endangered language, and a student seeking a new vocation after a successful trade career. Also, join us in celebrating a grad who landed an impressive new job and a professor who was tapped for a prestigious organization.
BRUMBERG NAMED INTERIM PRESIDENT
Joshua C. Brumberg, dean for the sciences and a longtime member of the CUNY family, will succeed President Robin L. Garrell. Read Chancellor Félix Matos Rodríguez’s announcement.
RECOGNIZING LEADERS OF HISPANIC SCHOLARSHIP
In celebration of Hispanic Heritage Month, we recognize our scholars who are leaders and pushing the boundaries of knowledge.
CHOOSING A CUNY TENURE TRACK
With multiple offers to choose from, alumnus Edwin Grimsley, whose experience includes working for the Innocence Project, is starting as an assistant professor at Baruch College this fall. He explains his choice and shares tips for others who aspire to get on the tenure track.
FROM TRADE SCHOOL TO A PSYCHOLOGY PH.D.
Sean Bennett’s first attempt at college was cut short. After a career in the air-conditioning business, he finished his undergraduate degree and is now starting a Ph.D. in Psychology. He shares his story and advice for older students.
DISCOVERING THE SECRETS OF SEA STARS
The study conducted by Graduate Center biologists, which reveals that two types of sea stars are mating in the North Atlantic, has implications for climate change.
SCHOLARSHIP TO SAVE A LANGUAGE 
Chiara Di Maio, a new Linguistics doctoral student, is trying to better understand and protect a dialect spoken on an island in the Venetian Lagoon.
FILMMAKER JOINS SOCIOLOGY PH.D. PROGRAM 
“To be compensated and surrounded by people encouraging you to read, write, and share about the things you care most about is nothing short of an honor,” said Sociology Ph.D. student Olivia Heffernan. 
TRAN ELECTED A MEMBER OF PRESTIGIOUS ASSOCIATION
His election to the Sociological Research Association, a rare achievement for an associate professor, is based on the strength of his scholarship, which focuses on immigrant integration.
REMEMBERING DISTINGUISHED PROFESSOR EMERITA FODOR
The prominent psycholinguist was a revered teacher and mentor. Several of her former students shared these tributes.

A PUBLIC GRADUATE SCHOOL IN THE CENTER OF NYC

The Graduate Center of The City University of New York, a leader in public graduate education, is committed to doctoral and master's education for the public good.

Innovative research, rigorous scholarship, and collaborative and interdisciplinary learning define graduate education here and prepare students with creative, problem-solving expertise to thrive in and beyond the academy.

We are dedicated to CUNY's historic mission of educating the "children of the whole people," providing access to graduate education for diverse and highly talented students, including those who have been underrepresented in higher education.
